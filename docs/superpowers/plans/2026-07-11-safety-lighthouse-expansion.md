# Safety Lighthouse Expansion Plan

**Goal:** Complete five child-safe digital-citizenship lessons covering password habits, private information, suspicious prompts, healthy computer use, and a whole-curriculum capstone.

## Safety rules

- Never ask for real names, passwords, phone numbers, addresses, school names, photos, or account details.
- All examples are fictional and stay in component memory only.
- “Ask a trusted adult” is a concrete action, not a vague warning.
- Safety questions avoid fear, shame, surveillance, or pretending children alone are responsible for sophisticated threats.

## Courses

1. `password-guardian`: build long fictional passphrases, keep them unique/private, and ask a caregiver about password managers.
2. `private-information`: sort fictional information by safe audience and practice pause/ask/close.
3. `popup-fog`: inspect sender, request, urgency, download, and destination before choosing close/ask/continue.
4. `healthy-computer-habits`: arrange posture, distance, light, breaks, and offline activity into a sustainable routine.
5. `light-bit-island`: combine file finding, keyboard/input, sequencing, loops, conditions, debugging, and safety decisions in a final rescue mission.

## Quality gates

- Pure decision-model tests plus accessible interaction components.
- No real credential fields, uploads, downloads, external links, or personal-data persistence.
- Full production tests, Pages artifact test, lint, live Pages workflow, and browser smoke test before final release.
